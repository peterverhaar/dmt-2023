Who I am and how I got here

Hi everyone and welcome to my introductory page! I'm Sara. I apologise in advance for the difficult last name, don't worry, I rarely use the weird letters here in the Netherlands and luckily my first name is a bit more manageable (thanks mum!). However, despite the dread in people's faces when they try to pronounce it, my name does indeed give away a lot about myself. I'm from Iceland - the country of "dóttir" and "son" and some strange letters other languages really don't bother with anymore. I grew up under quite different circumstances than my current living situation. For example, more people live in my current city (The Hague) than live in my whole country and I grew up on a farm with horses and sheep (and my lovely dog!). There are no trains but instead lots and lots of mountains where the Netherlands really can't compare. But with not so many people around, I grew up reading a lot and completely falling in love with the world of books. With some twists and turns in the road, I find myself still living in the Netherlands after having finished my undergraduate degree in International Studies and now I am hoping to continue to work with books and publishing for years to come which of course starts with the BDMS MA program.

BDMS starting point and expectations

Prior Knowledge I am somewhat of a blank page when it comes to digital media technology. This is my first encounter with HTML and CSS. However, I have worked on some projects in LaTeX format using Overleaf. It has given an insight into coding language and functions.
Expectations In this course I hope to learn the basics of coding language and how it can be used to create and transform digital texts. I hope to gain an understandings of the makeup of the digital platforms that are used in publishing, online archives etc. This course was for me one of the major selling points of the program as it equips us with hard skills that I'm sure will be valuable as we venture out into the big scary world of work.
